Beehiiv vs Substack: I Ran Both Newsletters for a Month — Here's What Actually Matters

May 26, 2026
Newsletter ToolsComparisonCreator EconomyAI Newsletters

Two months ago I got annoyed at my Substack dashboard and decided to run a side-by-side test. Same content, same posting schedule, two different platforms. One newsletter on Beehiiv, one on Substack. Thirty days later, the results were not what I expected.

Here's the short version: Substack wins on simplicity. Beehiiv wins on everything else that actually makes money. But it depends on what kind of creator you are — and I'll get into that.

The setup: what I actually tested

I publish two newsletters. One is a weekly roundup about AI tools for solopreneurs (about 2,000 subscribers). The other is a personal project about design and creative workflows (about 800 subscribers). I moved the AI tools one to Beehiiv and kept the creative one on Substack.

Same writing process for both: I draft in Claude, clean it up myself, add screenshots, hit publish. I did not use any platform's built-in AI writing features for the test — I wanted to compare the platforms, not the AI assistants.

Growth: Beehiiv absolutely crushed it

This is the part I didn't see coming. In 30 days, the Beehiiv newsletter added 340 subscribers. The Substack one added 67. Same content quality, same promotion effort.

The difference came from three things:

1. The Boosts referral system. Beehiiv's recommendation engine actually works. Other newsletters in my niche recommended mine, and it drove about 40% of the new subscribers. Substack has recommendations too, but they seem to favor already-large publications.

2. The magic link page. Beehiiv gives you a nice landing page to share that converts better than Substack's default subscribe page. It sounds small, but when you're dropping links on Twitter and Reddit, the conversion rate difference adds up fast.

3. The Ad Network. I ran $50 in Beehiiv ads promoting the newsletter inside other newsletters. Got 28 subscribers from that alone. Substack doesn't have anything comparable.

Growth winner: Beehiiv, by a lot. If you're starting from zero and need to build an audience, Substack's organic discovery isn't enough anymore in 2026.

AI features: both have them, Beehiiv's are better

Both platforms shoved AI into their editors this year. Substack's AI writing assistant is... fine. It'll help you write a subject line or clean up a paragraph. It's decent but feels bolted on.

Beehiiv's AI features go deeper. You can generate full newsletter drafts with different AI models, create custom AI images for headers, auto-generate subject line variants for A/B testing, and use AI to score how likely your subject line is to get opened.

The AI image generator is genuinely useful if you don't have a Canva design habit. I still use Canva for most graphics because I like having control, but for quick header images the Beehiiv AI tool is faster than opening a new tab.

What I actually found myself using most was the AI subject line tester. You paste in a few ideas and it scores them on predicted open rate. It's not magic, but it's directionally useful — the ones it scored highest did, in fact, perform better.

Neither platform's AI is replacing my actual writing workflow though. I still write in Claude, edit by hand, and use Perplexity for fact-checking and research. The platform AI is more of a polish layer.

Monetization: this is where Substack loses

Substack takes 10% of your paid subscription revenue. On a $10/month newsletter with 100 paid subscribers, that's $100/month out the door. On Beehiiv's Scale plan at $43/month, you keep everything.

The math shifts at scale. Cross roughly $430/month in paid revenue and Beehiiv's flat fee becomes cheaper than Substack's 10% cut. Most creators who are serious about monetizing will hit that threshold within 6-12 months.

Beehiiv also gives you the Ad Network on paid plans — you earn money by running ads in your newsletter even if you don't have paid subscribers yet. Substack has no equivalent.

Monetization winner: Beehiiv. If you plan to charge for your newsletter at any point, Substack's 10% cut is a slow leak in the boat.

Where Substack still wins

I need to give Substack credit where it deserves it.

The writing experience is cleaner. Substack's editor is distraction-free in a way Beehiiv's isn't. Beehiiv has more features but the interface is busier. When I just want to write, Substack feels better.

The community features are real. Substack Notes, the chat feature, the ability for readers to comment and discuss — this is where Substack is genuinely ahead. If your newsletter thrives on reader discussion, Substack's community layer is better than anything Beehiiv has.

The network effect is different. On Substack, readers are already logged in and browsing other newsletters. Cross-pollination happens naturally within the Substack ecosystem. Beehiiv newsletters feel more like standalone websites that happen to have email capabilities.

It's free forever. No subscriber cap on the free plan. If you're publishing for fun with zero monetization plans, Substack costs you nothing. Beehiiv caps at 2,500 free subscribers before you need to pay.

The workflow I landed on

After the test, I moved both newsletters to Beehiiv. Here's the actual stack I use to run them:

Writing: Draft in Claude, fact-check with Perplexity, final edit by hand.

Graphics: Canva for thumbnails and social images. Beehiiv's built-in AI image tool for quick headers when I'm lazy.

Publishing: Beehiiv for hosting, sending, and analytics.

Automation: Make handles the plumbing — when I publish, it auto-posts to Twitter, LinkedIn, and my Discord. Saves me about 15 minutes per issue.

Audio version: ElevenLabs generates an audio narration of each issue for subscribers who prefer listening. Takes about 3 minutes to generate and sounds better than most human voiceovers I've paid for.

Hosting & landing pages: Hostinger for the main website and any custom landing pages outside of Beehiiv.

Who should pick Substack

Go with Substack if:

  • You're writing for pleasure, not profit
  • Community discussion is central to your newsletter
  • You want a dead-simple editor with zero learning curve
  • You're already part of the Substack ecosystem and your audience lives there
  • You never plan to monetize beyond paid subscriptions

Who should pick Beehiiv

Go with Beehiiv if:

  • Growth is a priority and you want every tool available
  • You plan to monetize through ads, affiliates, or paid subs
  • You want actual analytics, not just open rates
  • You run multiple newsletters or plan to scale
  • You're okay trading a slightly busier UI for more features

The honest bottom line

I didn't want to like Beehiiv more. Moving newsletters is a pain. But after a month of running both, the difference in growth alone made the switch worth it. Substack's 10% cut felt reasonable when they were the only game in town. In 2026, it doesn't.

If I were starting a brand new newsletter tomorrow, I'd use Beehiiv from day one. The free plan gives you enough runway to figure out if the newsletter has legs, and when it does, you're already on the platform with the better monetization toolkit.

The only scenario where I'd still recommend Substack is if community is the entire point of what you're building. If reader discussion, Notes, and the Substack social layer are core to your value proposition — not just a nice-to-have — then Substack makes sense.

For everyone else: Beehiiv.
